View

MengQi Cheng is a graduate student at the School of Information Science and Engineering, Shandong Normal University. She received her bachelor's degree in Information Engineering from the same university and is currently pursuing her master's degree in computer science, with a focus on intelligent systems and data analysis.

Her research interests include machine learning, human-computer interaction, and educational technology. She is particularly focused on developing user-adaptive systems that improve learning outcomes and user engagement. In recent work, she has explored how behavioral patterns in digital learning environments can be used to personalize content delivery and enhance the overall learning experience.

MengQi has participated in several research projects related to smart education and data-driven decision-making, and has experience in Python programming, data visualization, and model evaluation. She is passionate about applying artificial intelligence techniques to solve practical problems in education and social sciences.

Projects

Projects

Participant: Intelligent Learning Behavior Analysis Platform

Project Dates: March 2024 – Present

Research Areas: Learning analytics, behavior modeling, adaptive recommendation

Project Description: This project focuses on analyzing behavioral data from online learning platforms to identify learning patterns and engagement levels. MengQi Cheng is responsible for designing data preprocessing pipelines and feature extraction methods to support behavior modeling and personalized feedback mechanisms. The goal is to improve learning outcomes through timely interventions and adaptive learning paths.

Participant: Privacy-Preserving Educational Data Mining

Project Dates: November 2023 – Present

Research Areas: Privacy protection, data mining, educational platforms

Project Description: In this interdisciplinary project, techniques such as differential privacy and federated learning are applied to analyze educational data without compromising user confidentiality. MengQi contributes to the development of privacy-aware data collection protocols and model training workflows. The project aims to build a secure infrastructure for educational research using real user data.

Academic Exploration: Personalized Learning Path Generation Using Reinforcement Learning

Project Dates: September 2023 – Present

Research Areas: Reinforcement learning, knowledge tracing, personalized education

Project Description: As part of her graduate thesis, MengQi is investigating the use of reinforcement learning algorithms to generate individualized learning paths for students. The study explores how student performance and engagement history can be used to guide content selection dynamically. The project is in the experimental stage, with simulations being conducted on synthetic and real student datasets.
